Dr. Amara Nkosi

Title: Digital Archaeologist

Dr. Amara Nkosi specializes in uncovering and preserving early game code, console hardware, and lost digital assets.

Dr. Amara Nkosi holds a PhD in Archaeogaming from MIT’s Comparative Media Studies program and a Master's in Digital Anthropology. Her work focuses on unearthing and emulating abandoned game environments from the early 1980s through the 2000s.

She leads the preservation and curation of "Lost Levels," an exhibit that reconstructs unreleased or region-locked games using digital artifacts and archival hardware.

Leon Rivera

Play Pattern Analyst

Leon Rivera focus on the cultural behavior and the humane interaction behind the game development and game communities

Leon Rivera holds a degree in Interactive Media Studies from UC Santa Cruz. He analyzes the evolution of game mechanics and avatar customization. At the museum, he manages the “Avatars of Us” exhibit, connecting player-created content to cultural storytelling.

Lyra Caldwell

Narrative Interpreter

Lyra Caldwell holds a Master’s in Game Narrative from NYU. she’s a published theorist and frequent speaker at GDC and the Museum of Play. Her focus includes analyzing symbols, power structures, and cultural metaphors in games ranging from indie titles to AAA franchises.

Lyra Caldwell is a cultural historian who explores how games transmit mythology, power structures, and identity. She collaborates with fan communities, historians, and modders to unpack meaning in pixelated worlds. Lyra contrasts oral storytelling traditions with branching RPG narratives.
